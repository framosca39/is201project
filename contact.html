Meet a Member

All the way from La Paz, Bolivia, learn about a dancer who recently joined the group!

My name is Fernando Ramos. I am a Pre-Business Student looking to apply to the Global Supply Chain Program with a minor in Spanish at Brigham Young Univerisity in Provo, UT. I am a first-generation college student.

I plan on using my major apply for a position in Boeing Airplane & Transport Corporation, in efforts to work in the Supply Chain Management Analyst team. As a back up plan, I would still like to be part of a supply chain team or consulting team for any other company that closely works with Spanish speaking countries. I was able to get the opportunity to begin dancing with this prestigious team that I’m currently in. Although I didn’t grow up dancing, I was fortunate enough to be taught mentors the importance of practice. Dance has taught me to be patient with myself and push myself to higher levels to thrive to portray the beauty of my Latin culture. Through my Latin culture, I am able To represent the joy through my yells and strength in my footwork. I’ve been able to share more about Bolivia’s traditional dance of Caporales which explain a portion of Bolivias historic Spaniard influence to the indigenous natives of the land that was conquered.

In addition to my gratitude to the education plan and dance culture that I’m in, I attribute my motivation and given purpose to strive to do better each day through the sacrifices made by my family, especially my mother. Recognizing the past, truly humbles a person if they seek to understand the details of our lineage. I find that finding strength through faith and hope in higher beings greater than ourselves, whether it’s mentors, family members, religious figures, or other leaders, can truly help us do better, and be better. All walks of life benefit from learning from someone who has experienced what we have yet to face for ourselves. With that, we can set higher goals and set better plans for our own lives.
